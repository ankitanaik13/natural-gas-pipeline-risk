Natural Gas Pipeline Risk Mapping & Incident Prediction

Geospatial ML · Energy Infrastructure · U.S. PHMSA 2002-2024

Project Overview

An end-to-end geospatial machine learning project that predicts the severity of U.S. natural gas pipeline incidents using federal PHMSA data spanning 2002-2024. The pipeline combines two incompatible PHMSA reporting schemas (3,047 incidents across 43 states), performs spatial joins to U.S. Census 2025 county geometries, and trains Random Forest and XGBoost classifiers with SHAP explainability to identify the features that drive high-severity outcomes.


The output includes a county-level choropleth, 1-mile population exposure buffers around high-severity incidents, and an interactive Folium risk map with ML-predicted risk scores layered over historical incident locations.

Research Question

Which U.S. natural gas pipeline segments are at highest risk of high-severity incidents, and can historical incident patterns, pipeline characteristics, and geospatial context predict risk before an incident occurs?

Geospatial Analysis

Key Findings

  • Corrosion and equipment failure are the leading causes, together accounting for over 60% of all events. Pipeline age and material type are the strongest structural predictors of future severity.
  • Pipelines installed in the 1950s-1970s show a disproportionately high incident rate, reflecting the risk posed by aging steel infrastructure installed before modern PHMSA integrity management requirements.
  • Texas and Louisiana account for the largest share of incidents due to dense Gulf Coast pipeline networks, while Appalachian states (WV, PA) show higher severity rates per incident.
  • SHAP analysis identifies pipeline age at incident, county incident history, and property damage cost as the three most predictive features, providing operators with interpretable and actionable risk signals.

Data Sources

  • Primary PHMSA Gas Transmission Incident Data - U.S. DOT Pipeline & Hazardous Materials Safety Administration. 2002-2024. Incident locations, causes, casualties, and property damage.
  • Spatial U.S. Census TIGER/Line County Shapefiles 2025 - County boundaries and FIPS codes used for spatial joins and choropleth mapping.
  • Pipeline National Pipeline Mapping System (NPMS) - Public pipeline route shapefiles by state (operator, commodity, geometry).
